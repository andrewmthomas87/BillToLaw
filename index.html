How a bill becomes a law

By Andrew Thomas

The beginning

Every law starts as an idea.

Ideas for laws can come from any citizen.

These ideas are given to the State's Representatives.

Representatives research these ideas and write bills based on them.

The proposal

The Representative shows the bill to other Representatives.

The Representative tries to find a sponsor for the bill.

Once a sponsor is found for the bill and several Representatives support the bill, the process moves forward.

The introduction

The Representatives supporting the bill introduce it to the House of Representatives.

To introduce the bill, they put it in the Hopper.

The bill is given a number and then sent to a standing committee.

At committee

Experts from the House of Representatives talk about the bill.

They read the bill, research the problems it addresses, and sometimes revise it.

Then they vote whether to send it back to the House floor or if the bill should die.

Debate on House floor

After the bill is approved by a committee, it is sent to the House floor.

Representatives debate the bill, explaining why they like or dislike it.

They make changes to each section of the bill to make it better.

Once all the changes have been made, they vote on the bill.

The vote

Each State Representative in presence votes on the bill.

If a majority vote for the bill, it passes.

If less than a majority vote for it, it dies.

On to the Senate

After being approved by the House of Representatives, the bill moves to the Senate.

The Senate does many of the same things as the House of Representatives to research and debate the bill.

Once satisfied that they know enough about the bill, they vote on it.

If a majority of Senators don't vote "yes" for the bill, it dies.

Final step! The President

Now that the bill has been approved by both the House of Representatives and the Senate, it goes to the President.

If the President likes the bill, he signs it and it becomes a law.

If the President doesn't like the bill, he can veto it.

If the President vetoes the bill, it goes back to the Senate who can override the veto and make the bill a law if 2/3 vote "yes".

The end

That's how a bill becomes a law
